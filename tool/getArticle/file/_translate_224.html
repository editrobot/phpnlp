澳大利亚体育组织联合起来打击非法赌博

作者: 博之道 来源: 未知 时间: 2011-09-06 阅读: 次 博彩译文

　　澳大利亚主要的体育团体 - 包括武装部队，澳大利亚板球，橄榄球联盟NRL和澳大利亚 - 已经联手，试图杜绝非法赌博。

　　该运动成立了调查小组，研究他们的选择，其中最激进的将是一个永久的“赌博委员会”。

　　该委员会，该委员会将成立由主要体育，博彩机构和澳大利亚体育委员会，将进行到投注法医调查。涉及其他运动包括网球，足球和无挡板篮球。

　　该消息是作为橄榄球和板球正面临博彩丑闻和警方正在调查最近对比赛暴跌。

　　对主要的专业和参与体育联盟 - 执政的身体在澳大利亚所有的大众参与的体育 - 启动了一个在体育赌博为5个月的调查。

　　COMPPS执行主任马尔科姆速度 - 世界板球的前老板 - 说，该调查的主要焦点是“异国情调”的“微型”体育博彩的结果。

　　“我们也为'看在播放的线上赌博投注及互动，”速度昨晚说。

　　调查的目的是要“在澳大利亚防弹职业体育 - 或接近它，可能从腐败 - ”。

　　高速表示，近期将嫌疑人的争论，包括坎特伯雷牛头犬在北昆士兰牛仔和海军研究实验所匹配在巴基斯坦和英国板球测试无球丑闻投注警方调查，是“对事物的那种我们在”寻找。

　　速度说COMPPS看着“在英格兰，在那里赌博委员会已经成立随着体育有关的具体立法体育欺骗”了最近的事态发展。

　　他说，赌博委员会“的众多选择之一”。最大限度地减少腐败的其他方式包括允许体育“管理自己的诚信和他们之间的体育”共享信息管理方案。也有可能存在“的博彩机构的信息共享方面”更广泛的合作，他说。

　　速度，谁成为COMPPS今年早些时候就职的头说，调查将在11月交付的初步报告。

　　海军研究实验所的首席执行官大卫盖洛普海军研究实验所证实，昨晚是“考虑对投注”，看看是否有一个集中的资源“的优点，以监察它的一些选项”。

　　“我们会在所有的芯片和我们就都有助于一个人谁了所有体育的作用，如果他们有一个特定的（赌博）的问题，”他说。

　　盖洛普说，他希望，如果赌博委员会进行了介绍，它woulduse就具体案件的行业专家。

　　他列举了海军研究实验所的首席管家悉尼赛车使用射线Murrihy调查犯罪嫌疑人投注在牛头犬，牛仔队比赛的趋势。

　　Sports unite to fight illegal betting

　　AUSTRALIA'S major sports bodies - including the AFL, Cricket Australia, NRL and Australian Rugby Union - have joined forces to try to stamp out illegal betting.

　　The sports have formed an investigative unit to examine their options, the most radical of which would be a permanent "gambling commission".

　　The commission, which would be jointly established by major sports, betting agencies and the Australian Sports Commission, would carry out forensic investigations into betting. Other sports involved include tennis, soccer and netball.

　　The news comes as rugby league and cricket are facing betting scandals and police are investigating recent plunges on matches.

　　The Coalition of Major Professional and Participation Sports - the body governing all mass-participation sports in Australia - has launched a five-month investigation into gambling in sport.

　　COMPPS executive director Malcolm Speed - the former boss of world cricket - said the main focus of the investigation was "exotic" betting on "micro" outcomes in sports.

　　"We're also looking at 'in-play' betting with online and interactive gambling," Speed said last night.

　　The investigation was designed to "make professional sport in Australia bulletproof - or as close to it as possible - from corruption".

　　Speed said recent controversies including the police investigation into suspect betting on the Canterbury Bulldogs-North Queensland Cowboys NRL match and the no-ball scandal in the Pakistan-England cricket Test, were "the sort of things we're looking at".

　　Speed said COMPPS was watching "recent developments in England, where a gambling commission has been set up along with sports-specific legislation relating to cheating in sport".

　　He said a gambling commission was "one of many options". Other ways of minimising corruption include allowing sports to "run their own integrity and regulatory programs that they share information among the sports". There could also be "wider co-operation with betting agencies in terms of sharing information", he said.

　　Speed, who became the inaugural head of COMPPS earlier this year, said the investigation would deliver a preliminary report by November.

　　NRL chief executive David Gallop last night confirmed the NRL was "considering some options" on betting "to see if there's merit in a centralised resource" to monitor it.

　　"We'd all chip in and we'd all contribute to a person who had a role across all the sports if they had a particular (betting) issue," he said.

　　Gallop said he hoped that, if a gambling commission were introduced, it woulduse industry experts on specific cases.

　　He cited the NRL's use of chief Sydney racing steward Ray Murrihy to investigate the suspect betting trends in the Bulldogs-Cowboys match.
