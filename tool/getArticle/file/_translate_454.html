扑克之星推出世界在线扑克锦标赛

作者: 博之道 来源: 未知 时间: 2011-09-27 阅读: 次 博彩译文

　　多年来为人们所熟知，你不需要现场参加扑克锦标赛，在线参加扑克锦标赛也能赚得大钱。

　　虽然以往任何时候都有更多的机会在线比赛，但本周没有在线扑克世界锦标赛揭开序幕。

　　现在进入第10个年头的扑克之星的活动的奖金吸引了超过任何其他在线活动更多的玩家。

　　9月4日开始的比赛将持续到9月25日，在此期间举行了大规模的62个不同的比赛事件。

　　这些比赛事件的奖金总额将达$ 3,000,000！

　　这将是地球上任何地方任何网站上提供的在线扑克的最高额的奖金。

　　对于第一次在线扑克世界锦标赛，整个过程将伴随着一个特殊的电台直播节目，让你日程的所有安排，有所行动。

　　扑克之星的无线电波将全天24小时覆盖广播，并于9月7日开始播放。 每周直播六天，一直到9月27日。

　　覆盖范围将包括所有重要的新闻和玩家访谈，以及派发跨越四种语言广播的奖品50,000元（英语，俄语，德语和西班牙语）。

　　Pokerstars Launches World Championship Of Online Poker

　　by ryan | Published: Thursday, September 8th, 2011

　　For years now it been well known that you don need to play live tournament poker to win big money with the online poker realm exploding.

　　While there are more opportunities than ever online, there are none that match up to the World Championship of Online Poker which kicked off this week.

　　Now into its 10th year the  Pokerstars event attracts more players for more prize money than any other online event.

　　Starting on September 4th the tournaments will progress right through to September 25th with a massive 62 different events held during that period.

　　If thats not enough to get you keen then how about the fact the total amount of prize money which will be given away will total a guaranteed $3,000,000!

　　That makes it the richest prize pool offered for online poker on any website anywhere on the planet.

　　The best part about it is that you don  need to be a high roller to get your start in the events either with hundreds of satellite tournaments offering players the chance to earn their way in from as little as $1 or even free with 100 frequent player points.

　　For the first time ever as well the entire World Championship of Online Poker will be accompanied with a special live radio show to keep you up to date with all the action.

　　Pokerstars radio will be covering the days play with hour long broadcasts starting on September 7th.

　　They l run six days a week all the way through to September 27th.

　　The coverage will include all the important news and player interviews as well as handing out $50,000 in prizes across four language broadcasts （English, Russian, German and Spanish）。
